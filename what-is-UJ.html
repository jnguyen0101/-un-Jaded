We're (un)Jaded

A mental health and service organization intended to destigmatize mental health and encourage social advocacy amongst the AAPI community. (un)Jaded recognizes the need for a culturally aware space that is committed to celebrating Asian identities, addressing intersectional issues, and promoting greater community involvement. Membership is open to self-identified Asians, Pacific Islanders, Asian Americans, and allies attending the University of Texas at Austin.

Meet the Founders

×

Abby Ong

(un)Jaded will forever occupy a soft spot in my heart — Kai-Lin, J, and I started it as a simple desire for community, but it grew rapidly far beyond us. I think its success is a testament to the need for open conversation and community building among AAPI, particularly to advance mental health and social justice. My favorite part of the organization was always just talking in circles after one of the workshops, getting to know people in a way that required some vulnerability.

(un)Jaded always attracted the best people, and in it, I found some lifelong friends. People have told me that finding (un)Jaded was what helped get them through the social isolation of COVID and college. As the organization continues to grow and change, I hope it always occupies a space at UT driven to provide such a community.

×

Kai-Lin Wei

(un)Jaded was inspired by the countless conversations between Abby, J, and myself surrounding belonging, wellness, and connection among AAPI students at UT. We were proud of our heritages but bonded over the way certain traditional values or generational gaps in cultural understanding could chafe up against our American upbringing. As freshmen in 2019, we were all actively searching for AAPI communities on campus but couldn't find one that specifically focused on building the skills to navigate these tensions. The vision was to not only bring AAPI students together, but create a highly intentional space where vulnerabilities could be shared and true community built.

A beautiful community, incredible learning experiences, and lifelong relationships have come out of this project. Abby and J are, to this day, the best friends I continuously look to for advice and inspiration. We're pursuing different careers, living in different cities, and chasing different dreams — on my end, I'm wrapping up a cross-country move from San Francisco to start a new role at Google New York this summer — but I think we still all fundamentally care about the same things. We're inspired by our people. We're committed to carrying our histories with pride while still feeling empowered to explore new paths to prosperity. And we're thrilled to see what the next generation of AAPI Americans will bring to our ever-evolving culture. At this point, we're ready to learn from you!

×

Jannel Hayden

Loud. Opinionated. Argumentative. These were the words used to describe me, but always with some negative connotation. Eventually, I fell into the pattern of taking up less space, catering to the comfort of those who didn't care about what I had to say. But the truth is, some things just need to be heard, and often times, the voices that are silenced are the ones that challenge the systems keeping us all quiet in the first place.

When we push ourselves to have these difficult conversations about race, privilege, and social equality, we open doors for growth and create power in community. Conversation is the first step toward transformation, and creating a platform for AAPI voices to be heard is my way of fighting back.

I'm here to take up space.

Our Values

Mental Health

Radical wellness.

  • Destigmatizing mental health in the AAPI community and beyond through research and education.
  • Providing affordable, accessible, and diversified resources for all.

Social Advocacy

Open conversation.

  • Addressing community disparities and tackling concepts such as the perpetual foreigner and the model minority myth.
  • Speaking up to support other racial and ethnic minorities, intersectional groups, sexual and gender minorities, and mixed identities.

Community Outreach

Good work.

  • Providing real opportunities to engage with AAPI communities on- and off-campus, especially fundraising and volunteering.
  • Establishing partnerships with homegrown, ethical, and AAPI-owned brands and businesses.

Radical wellness. Open conversation. Good work.
